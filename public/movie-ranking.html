Sharah's Top Movies

My Favorite Flicks

Jackie Brown

Another classic from Quentin Tarantino. It was the first film of his that starred a black female lead
and I loved all the action happening. This lyric from a song springs to mind every time I think of this movie,
for some reason:

"Don't drink that wine,
coke it is better,
it's easy to find,
if that won't do,
smoke a joint or two, but don't drink that wine"

Blues Brothers

This movie is before my time, but my gosh did it have swagger for its time and tons of music.
It stars Dan Ackroyd and has an ensemble cast to back it too. I think this was the movie that made
me appreciate sing-songy movies, because generally I did not like them.

Death Becomes Her

Is this Meryl Streep's funniest role? Is this Bruce Willis' best supporting role? And do we agree this is Goldie
Hawn's best work? Whether or not, I love everything about this movie and have watched it several times to boot.
I wonder what I'd think of it if I watched it in the more enlightened technological age?

Reservoir Dogs

This is probably the movie that made me fall in love with Quentin Tarantino, his storytelling
and his style of films. I loved that rather than reflecting 1 storyline that spans a longer period of time,
he reflected the fact that SERIOUSLY FUNNY STUFF was happening to each character at the very same
moment in time and that it was in the coming together at the end that we'd get the most enjoyment.
